## 2. C1 — DAPLE (Diploma Avançado de Português Língua Estrangeira)

### General Information
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Time** | 10h45–12h15 (LAPE local time) |
| **Duration** | **90 minutes** |
| **Pages** | 2-page exam booklet |
| **Answer sheets** | 5 pages (2 for Part 1, 2 for Part 2, 1 for Part 3) |
| **Writing instrument** | Blue or black pen (no correction fluid/tape allowed) |
| **Word count** | 200–230 words per text (Part 1 & Part 2) |

### Format — 3 Parts

| Part | Task Type | Word Count | Items |
|------|-----------|------------|-------|
| **Part 1** | Formal letter/e-mail | 200–230 words | 1 text (mandatory) |
| **Part 2** | Text from 3 topics (choose 1) | 200–230 words | 1 text (choose from 3) |
| **Part 3** | Sentence rewriting | N/A | 10 sentences |

### Part 1 — Formal Letter
- **Genre**: Carta formal / e-mail formal
- **Functions**: Giving information, requesting, complaining, correcting information, persuading, arguing
- **Key requirements**:
  - Include all points specified in the prompt
  - Follow formal letter conventions (opening/closing formulas)
  - Address and date lines are NOT required (but their positions should be respected)
  - DO NOT identify the candidate personally
- **Evaluated on**: Ability to include required information clearly, coherently, and well-organized; adherence to genre conventions

### Part 2 — Text from Topics
- **Stimulus**: 3 topics/statements on current affairs (environment, lifestyles, human relations, technology, work, travel, entertainment, society, etc.)
- **Candidate choice**: Choose 1 of 3 topics
- **Text subtypes** (depending on topic):
  1. **Relato** (Narrative account) — 1st person, past tenses, chronological order, vivid details
  2. **Texto de opinião** (Opinion text) — Express agreement/disagreement, 1st person, future tenses
  3. **Texto argumentativo** (Argumentative text) — Present a thesis, support with evidence, 3rd person, persuade the reader
  4. **Compare/contrast** arguments or opinions
  5. **Propose and justify** solutions to a problem
- **Key requirements**: Coherent and cohesive text; varied vocabulary; effective paragraphing and connectors

### Part 3 — Sentence Rewriting
- **Items**: 10 sentences
- **Task**: Rewrite each sentence starting with the given word(s) while preserving the exact original meaning
- **Grammar focus**: Tests grammatical knowledge at C1 level (Referencial Camões — Grammar Inventory for C1)
- **Common strategies tested**:
  - Word class change (verb → adjective)
  - Tense/mood changes
  - Active ↔ passive voice
  - Clause inversion
  - Simple → compound tense
  - Direct ↔ indirect speech
  - Word ↔ phrase substitution
- **Scoring**: Different points for incorrect, partially correct, or fully correct answers
- **Warning**: Formally correct but semantically different answers are penalized

### Recommended Time Allocation (within 90 min)
| Activity | Recommended Time |
|----------|-----------------|
| Part 1 | ~25 min |
| Part 2 | ~25 min |
| Part 3 | ~25 min |
| Verification/Review | **15 min** |

### Evaluation / Classification Criteria

- **Scale**: 0–5 (applied to each of 3 parameters)
- **Evaluators**: 2 centrally trained CAPLE evaluators
- **3 Parameters**:
  1. **Adequação** (Adequacy — task completion, content, communicative effectiveness)
  2. **Correção/Precisão gramatical** (Grammatical accuracy — syntax, morphology, spelling)
  3. **Organização discursiva** (Discourse organization — coherence, cohesion, paragraphing)

- **Specific aspects evaluated** (detailed descriptors known only to evaluators):
  - Content & communicative effectiveness
  - Grammatical accuracy
  - Cohesion & coherence
  - Lexical control
  - Spelling/orthography
  - Idea development

- **Important**: Minor errors in spelling, grammar, or punctuation that **do not impede communication** do not necessarily affect the score.

- **Graded example from the guide** (formal letter — Part 1):
  - Score: **4 out of 5**
  - Comment: "Well-organized text. Good use of cohesion mechanisms. Three minor errors do not affect good command of syntactic structures. Good vocabulary control. Good impact on reader."
  - What earned the score: varied connectors (Portanto, Todavia, Por isso), technical vocabulary (carta registada, reembolsar), all required topics addressed

### Stimulus Types
- **Part 1**: Written task description
- **Part 2**: Statement/general question on current topic
- **Part 3**: Isolated sentences for rewriting

### Orthography Rules
- Either pre-1990 or post-1990 AO is accepted, as long as used **systematically and coherently**
- Brazilian Portuguese orthographic norms are also accepted if used **systematically and coherently**

## 3. C2 — DUPLE (Diploma Universitário de Português Língua Estrangeira)

### General Information
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Duration** | **105 minutes** |
| **Pages** | 2-page exam booklet |
| **Parts** | 3 parts |

### Format — 3 Parts

| Part | Task Type | Word Count | Detail |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Part 1** | Formal letter (complaint + proposal) | **220–250 words** | Describes situation, proposes solutions, requests compensation |
| **Part 2** | Text from 3 topics (choose 1) | **250–280 words** | Opinion/argumentative essay on abstract/cultural topics |
| **Part 3** | Sentence rewriting | N/A | 10 sentences |

### Part 1 — Formal Letter (C2)
- Example topic: Neighbors file a complaint about graffiti; describes current situation, proposes solutions, requests compensation
- **Word count**: 220–250 words (higher than DAPLE's 200–230)

### Part 2 — Text from Topics (C2)
- **Word count**: 250–280 words (higher than DAPLE's 200–230)
- **Topics are more abstract/philosophical** compared to DAPLE:
  - *Example A*: Cultural heritage as a transversal citizenship issue — expanding concept of heritage
  - *Example B*: Global village vs. loss of identity — consumerism and travel
  - *Example C*: Tourist vs. traveler — one-way ticket mindset

### Part 3 — Sentence Rewriting (C2)
- 10 sentences, same format as DAPLE
- Tests advanced C2 grammatical structures and transformations
- Example items test: passive constructions, conditional clauses, concessive structures, prepositional phrases, relative clauses

### Comparison: DAPLE vs DUPLE PIE

| Aspect | DAPLE (C1) | DUPLE (C2) |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **Duration** | 90 min | 105 min |
| **Part 1 word count** | 200–230 | 220–250 |
| **Part 2 word count** | 200–230 | 250–280 |
| **Part 3** | 10 sentences | 10 sentences |
| **Topic complexity** | Current affairs, social issues | Abstract, philosophical, cultural |
| **Parts** | 3 | 3 |
